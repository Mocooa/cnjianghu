**国潮 (guó cháo)** means "national trend" or "national tide." It names a market and cultural shift where Chinese-origin aesthetics are used in modern consumer products — fashion, cosmetics, food packaging, café interiors — without feeling old-fashioned.

**Why it is not just nationalism:** 国潮 works through design, texture, and mood rather than slogans. A tea brand using Song-dynasty color palettes. A sportswear line mixing calligraphy with streetwear. A café interior that feels Chinese and contemporary at the same time. The products succeed commercially because they look good, not just because they wave a flag.

**What changed:** A decade ago, Chinese consumers often preferred foreign brands for prestige. 国潮 marks the reversal. Brands like Li-Ning, Florasis, and Heytea built their identity on Chinese references that younger consumers find genuinely appealing rather than obligatory.

**Cultural context:** 国潮 sits alongside 新中式 (new Chinese style) and the hanfu revival. Together they signal a generation comfortable claiming Chinese aesthetic traditions as living, usable, commercially viable material.

**How to use it:** "That packaging is very 国潮 — traditional motifs but modern execution."
