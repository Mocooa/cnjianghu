**出海 (chū hǎi)** literally means "to go out to sea." In Chinese business language, it is the standard term for companies expanding internationally — and it is one of the most-discussed strategies in China's tech and manufacturing sectors right now.

**Who is going out to sea:** TikTok, Shein, Temu, BYD, Pop Mart, MiHoYo (Genshin Impact), DeepSeek. The wave spans social media, e-commerce, electric vehicles, designer toys, gaming, and AI. What they share is not industry but direction — outward.

**Why the metaphor works:** 出海 carries a sense of voyage, risk, and ambition. It is not a neutral word like "internationalization." It evokes navigation, discovery, and the uncertainty of foreign waters. Companies that 出海 are not just exporting — they are staking claims.

**What makes it different now:** Earlier waves of Chinese globalization sold cheap goods under foreign brands. The current 出海 wave sells branded products directly to consumers. Temu is not hiding its Chinese origin. BYD is competing with BMW. The posture has changed.

**How to use it:** "Pop Mart's 出海 strategy is working — there's a store in every major city now."
