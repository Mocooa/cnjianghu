**躺平 (tǎng píng)** literally means "to lie flat." It describes a deliberate withdrawal from China's relentless competition — choosing not to chase promotions, property, or marriage pressure.

**Why it matters:** 躺平 is the mirror image of 内卷 (neijuan). If 内卷 describes a race where everyone runs faster but nobody gets ahead, 躺平 is the decision to stop running entirely. It is not laziness. It is a refusal to keep playing a game with diminishing returns.

**How it started:** The term went viral in 2021 when a factory worker posted about quitting his job to live simply, spending as little as possible. "Lying flat is my wise movement," he wrote. The post was censored, which only made the idea spread faster.

**Cultural context:** 躺平 belongs to a trio of generational buzzwords: 内卷 (involution), 躺平 (lying flat), and 摆烂 (bǎi làn, "letting it rot"). Together they trace a mood arc — from exhausted competition, to quiet refusal, to ironic surrender.

**How to use it:** "She quit her 996 job and moved to Dali. Full 躺平 mode."
