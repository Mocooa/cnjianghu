**外卖 (wài mài)** literally means "outside selling." In practice, it means food delivery by app — primarily through Meituan or Ele.me — and it has become one of the most visible rhythms of Chinese urban life.

**Scale of impact:** In most Chinese cities, you can order from virtually any restaurant and have food arrive in 20-40 minutes. Riders on electric scooters are as common as taxis. Office buildings have dedicated pickup shelves. Entire restaurant categories exist that have no dine-in space at all.

**What it changed:** 外卖 did not just add convenience. It restructured how people eat. Cooking at home dropped. Solo dining became frictionless. Late-night eating expanded. And a new labor class — the 骑手 (rider) — became central to urban life and public debate about working conditions.

**Cultural context:** For many young workers in Chinese cities, 外卖 is not a luxury. It is the default. The phrase 点外卖 (to order delivery) is as routine as "making coffee" might be elsewhere.

**How to use it:** "Too tired to cook — let's just 点个外卖."
