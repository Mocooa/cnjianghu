**内卷 (nèi juǎn)** literally means "involution" — the opposite of evolution. It describes a situation where increasing effort produces diminishing returns because everyone is caught in the same escalation.

**Real-world example:** Imagine a movie theater where one person stands up to see better. Then the person behind them stands up. Soon everyone is standing, but nobody sees better than when they were all sitting. That's 内卷.

**Why it went viral:** In 2020, a photo of a Tsinghua University student coding on a laptop while riding a stationary bike went viral on Chinese social media. The caption? 内卷. It crystallized what millions of young Chinese already felt — the exhaustion of hyper-competition in education, work, and life.

**Cultural context:** 内卷 is the mirror image of 躺平 (tǎng píng, "lying flat"). One is the disease, the other is the cure — or perhaps the surrender.

**How to use it:** "996 work culture? That's pure 内卷."
