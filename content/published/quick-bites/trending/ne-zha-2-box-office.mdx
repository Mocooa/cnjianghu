**What happened:** 哪吒之魔童闹海 (Ne Zha 2) shattered box office records in early 2025, surpassing $2 billion in global revenue to become the highest-grossing non-English film in history. Almost all of that revenue came from China's domestic market.

**Why it matters:** Ne Zha 2 proved that a single national market can produce Hollywood-scale box office numbers. China does not need international distribution to break global records. The domestic audience alone is large enough — and willing to show up.

**The cultural context:** Ne Zha (哪吒) is a mythological figure from Chinese folklore — a rebellious child deity who defies his father and fights dragons. The 2019 original reimagined him as a punk underdog. The sequel deepened the mythology while delivering Pixar-level animation quality. For Chinese audiences, it is both spectacle and cultural pride.

**What outsiders miss:** Western media covered the box office numbers but rarely engaged with why the film resonated. Ne Zha is not a generic superhero. He embodies a specifically Chinese tension — between filial duty and individual rebellion, between fate and self-determination. That tension is why the audience returns.

**The industry shift:** China's animation industry has gone from imitating Disney to producing original mythological epics that rival anything in global animation. Ne Zha 2 is not a fluke. It is a signal.
