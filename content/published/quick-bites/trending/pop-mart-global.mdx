**What happened:** Pop Mart, the Chinese designer toy company, has been opening stores across Southeast Asia, Europe, and North America — and they are packed. Bangkok, Seoul, London, Los Angeles. The signature Labubu character hangs from backpacks everywhere. International revenue is growing faster than domestic.

**Why it is trending:** Pop Mart's expansion is not just commercial success. It is one of the few cases where a Chinese consumer brand has achieved genuine cultural desirability abroad — not competing on price, but on taste. Customers are not buying Pop Mart because it is cheap. They are buying it because they want it.

**What makes it work:** The figures are designed by independent artists, not derived from movie franchises. The blind-box mechanic creates collecting behavior and social sharing. The stores are beautifully designed. And the price point — $10-15 per box — makes it an impulse buy that feels like a small luxury.

**The bigger picture:** For years, Chinese companies that went global competed on cost (Shein, Temu) or technology (TikTok, DeepSeek). Pop Mart competes on aesthetic appeal. That is a different kind of 出海 — one that changes how "Made in China" is perceived.

**Why it matters:** Pop Mart proves that Chinese brands can export culture, not just products. The Labubu on a Parisian teenager's bag is doing more for China's soft power than any government initiative.
