## What people usually mean

骑手 usually refers to the workers who move through cities on scooters or e-bikes to deliver food, groceries, or parcels.

## Why the term matters

In Chinese cities, the 骑手 is not just a worker hidden behind an app. The role is highly visible:

- outside office towers
- at apartment compounds
- near pickup shelves
- in the timing logic of lunch and late-night food

## What outsiders often miss

`Rider` can sound like a simple job label. In China, 骑手 has become one of the most visible human interfaces of platform infrastructure.

## Why it matters

If instant delivery feels omnipresent in China, 骑手 helps explain why: the system is not abstract. It is constantly crossing your field of vision.
