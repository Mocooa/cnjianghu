## What this page is

`Factory-Direct` here is a **concept note**, not a claim that one single Chinese phrase cleanly covers every version of the idea.

It helps explain why foreign shoppers increasingly talk about buying "closer to the source" when they buy from China.

## What people usually mean in this archive context

In this context, `Factory-Direct` often points to:

- fewer brand or retail layers between factory and buyer
- lower prices framed as supply-chain efficiency
- curiosity about where an object actually comes from
- a shift from buying a finished brand story to buying closer to production itself

That is why the phrase often appears together with platform, logistics, and manufacturing curiosity.

## What outsiders often miss

This is not only about paying less. `Factory-Direct` often changes the whole meaning of the purchase.

It makes people ask different questions:

- Who really made this?
- Why is the same object priced differently under a retail brand?
- What part of the cost is production, and what part is distribution or branding?

## What we should not overclaim

Not every product marketed this way is literally shipped straight from one factory to one buyer. The phrase often works as a reader-facing shortcut for reduced middle layers and perceived source proximity.

But it remains useful because it explains why direct-from-China buying can feel like supply-chain discovery, not just bargain hunting.
