## Where the word came from

对账 originally belongs to bookkeeping and reconciliation. It means matching accounts and checking whether the numbers line up.

## What it means online now

In internet use, 对账 has widened. People use it when they:

- compare experiences across apps
- line up screenshots from different sides
- check whether one group's story matches another's
- compare what Chinese and foreign users are seeing

So the word keeps its old logic of verification, but moves into social and platform comparison.

## Why it became useful

In cross-platform and cross-border internet talk, 对账 is a concise way to say: "Let's compare what each side is actually seeing." That is why it feels sharper than simply saying "compare notes."

## What outsiders often miss

This is not just a literal accounting metaphor. Online Chinese use turns 对账 into a social act of cross-checking reality. It often carries a mild suspicion that two systems, two audiences, or two narratives are not aligned.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand how Chinese internet users talk when they compare app cultures or information environments, 对账 is one of the clearest live words in that space.
