## What this page is

`Internal Heat` works best here as a **concept note** rather than a single everyday catchword. It helps foreign readers understand the broader heat-based body logic that sits behind terms like 上火 and related wellness advice.

## What people usually mean in this archive context

In Chinese everyday reasoning, internal heat can point to a body that feels:

- irritated
- too stimulated
- out of balance after certain foods, stress, or lack of sleep
- in need of cooling, calming, or gentler routines

That is why people may respond with food, tea, rest, or schedule adjustments rather than with abstract explanation alone.

## What outsiders often miss

This is not best read as a precise biomedical category. It is a reader-facing bridge into a family of Chinese body concepts where heat is not just temperature, but a way of describing internal condition and imbalance.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese health talk often sounds thermal, relational, and practical all at once, internal heat is one of the most useful bridge concepts.
