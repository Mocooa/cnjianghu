## What kind of word this is

养生 is not just a label for "wellness content." It describes an orientation toward life: keep the body in working order, do not wait until things are clearly wrong, and prefer steady maintenance over dramatic intervention.

## What people usually mean

In ordinary life, 养生 can include:

- sleeping earlier
- adjusting food by season
- drinking certain teas or soups
- keeping the body warm
- avoiding overwork
- building small protective routines

It is often quiet, repetitive, and intentionally unshowy.

## Where it appears

- Parents and grandparents giving advice
- Office workers talking about tea, sleep, or fatigue
- Social media about routine health habits
- Small products that sit between food, remedy, and lifestyle

## What outsiders often miss

养生 is not just for older people, and it is not Chinese biohacking. It is a maintenance logic. The point is not optimization or peak performance, but long-term steadiness.

## Why it matters

养生 helps explain why so much Chinese health talk sounds ordinary rather than technical. In this worldview, prevention belongs inside daily life, not outside it.
