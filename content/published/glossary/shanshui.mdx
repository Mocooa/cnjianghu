## Beyond the literal

山水 literally means "mountains and waters," but it is not just a way to describe scenery. It is one of the deepest organizing ideas in Chinese visual culture.

## What people usually mean

Depending on context, 山水 can point to:

- landscape painting
- a certain atmosphere of distance and mist
- a way of arranging space
- a visual mood associated with classical China

## Where it appears

- painting and calligraphy
- garden design
- fantasy game environments
- film and drama art direction
- design language that wants to feel recognizably Chinese

## What outsiders often miss

山水 is not simply "nature" or "landscape." It carries an aesthetic logic about scale, emptiness, rhythm, and the relation between people and the world.

## Why it matters

When foreigners say Chinese fantasy looks different, 山水 is often one of the hidden reasons. It shapes not just what is shown, but how space itself is imagined.
