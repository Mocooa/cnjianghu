## What people usually mean

жҷЁз»ғ refers to the habit of doing exercise in the morning, often in public space. It can include walking, stretching, dancing, tai chi, equipment use, or light group routines.

## Why it feels culturally bigger than exercise alone

In China, жҷЁз»ғ often carries more than fitness logic. It can also mean:

- showing up in a public rhythm
- maintaining health through routine
- meeting familiar faces
- starting the day with visible order

That is why it often feels closer to habit and social infrastructure than to a purely individual workout goal.

## What outsiders often miss

жҷЁз»ғ is not just "people exercising in the morning." In many Chinese cities it is one of the most visible forms of ordinary public life, especially among older adults, and it helps explain why parks often feel socially active instead of empty.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese public parks often feel communal before they feel scenic, жҷЁз»ғ is one of the clearest live terms.
