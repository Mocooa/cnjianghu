## What it is

ĺĽąĺą• are comments that appear directly across the video screen at the specific moment a viewer posts them. They are tied to the timeline, not just placed below the video.

## What it changes

Because comments are time-synced, viewers respond together:

- they react to the same scene at the same second
- they warn each other
- they repeat familiar jokes
- they signal shared feeling
- they make solitary viewing feel collective

This is why ĺĽąĺą• are especially associated with Bilibili and with online cultures where co-watching matters.

## How people actually use it

- to joke at a famous scene
- to mark an emotional moment
- to "arrive" together at a well-known timestamp
- to create company while watching alone

## What outsiders often miss

ĺĽąĺą• are not just comments floating on video. They are part of the viewing mechanism itself. In Chinese internet culture, they help turn a video into a room full of simultaneous reactions.

## Why it matters

If you want to understand how Chinese online life feels socially dense, ĺĽąĺą• is one of the clearest examples. A platform feature became a cultural habit.
