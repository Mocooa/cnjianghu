## What it means

借景 literally means borrowing scenery. In Chinese design thinking, it refers to making a distant view, neighboring element, or framed outside scene feel like part of the space you are in.

## How it works

This can happen through:

- windows and openings
- framed sight lines
- garden walls and corridors
- the placement of pavilions, rocks, water, or plants

The point is not simply to show more. It is to reshape how space is experienced.

## What outsiders often miss

Borrowed scenery is not just decorative cleverness. It reflects a deeper Chinese spatial logic in which boundaries can be softened, layered, or made porous through framing and movement.

## Why it matters

If foreign readers want to understand why Chinese gardens, films, and even some contemporary interiors can feel unusually composed and atmospheric, 借景 is one of the most useful concepts to know.
