## What people usually mean

情绪价值 is now widely used in Chinese consumer and social language to describe something that feels worth it because of what it does for your mood, not just because of its practical function.

People use it for:

- cute objects
- friendships and relationships
- services that feel attentive
- purchases that make daily life feel softer or more manageable

## Why the phrase spread

The term became useful because it gave people a way to justify choices that are not strictly rational in cost-benefit terms but still feel genuinely necessary in emotional life.

## What outsiders often miss

This is not simply an excuse for impulse spending. In Chinese use, 情绪价值 often points to a serious question: what helps you endure pressure, maintain mood, or make ordinary life feel livable?

## Why it matters

If you want to understand why collectibles, plush charms, desk toys, or small ritual purchases can feel important in contemporary China, 情绪价值 is one of the clearest explanatory phrases.
