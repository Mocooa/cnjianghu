## More than body type

体质 is often translated as body constitution. The phrase usually points to the idea that people are not all starting from the same bodily baseline, so the same food, weather, routine, or treatment may not suit everyone equally.

## What people usually mean

When people talk about 体质, they are often referring to:

- a body's recurring tendencies
- sensitivity to heat, cold, dampness, fatigue, or certain foods
- why one person should "take care" differently from another

That is why the term often sounds intuitive in Chinese daily health talk.

## What outsiders often miss

体质 is not simply physique, weight, or modern medical diagnosis. It is a way of talking about patterned bodily difference. Even people who do not speak in formal TCM language may still reason through it.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese wellness advice often sounds personalized through patterns rather than universal through rules, 体质 is one of the clearest entry terms.
