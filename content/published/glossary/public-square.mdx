## More than a location

广场 literally means a square or plaza, but in Chinese urban life it often means more than a piece of built space. It can act as a reliable social stage.

## What happens there

- group dancing
- children's play
- evening strolling
- informal gathering
- public waiting and resting
- seasonal events and neighborhood activity

## What outsiders often miss

Foreign readers may imagine a public square mainly as a political symbol, tourist landmark, or transit node. In China it is often also a routine leisure surface where ordinary collective life becomes visible.

## Why it matters

If you want to understand why public leisure in China looks so present, 广场 is one of the basic spaces that makes that visibility possible.
