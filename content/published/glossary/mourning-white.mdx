## More than "white means bad"

缟素 is a classical term associated with mourning dress in white or undyed cloth. It helps explain one important layer of Chinese color logic: white can carry funerary and grief associations even though it can also appear elegant, minimal, or refined in other settings.

## What people usually mean in this archive context

Here, "mourning white" does not mean that every use of white in China is negative. It points to the specific ritual and cultural memory that makes white feel sensitive in some contexts, especially around death, memorial practice, and solemnity.

## What outsiders often miss

Foreign readers sometimes learn that red is lucky and white is unlucky, then stop there. That is too blunt. White in China is not culturally forbidden. But because of its long association with mourning, its meaning is more context-sensitive than many outsiders expect.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese color use can feel symbolic before it feels decorative, mourning white is one of the most important counterweights to auspicious red.
