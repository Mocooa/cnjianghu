## What people usually mean

In Chinese daily speech, 物业 usually means the property-management system attached to a residential compound or building complex. It can refer to the company, the office, the staff at the gate, or the whole service layer residents deal with.

## What it actually does

For many urban residents, 物业 is involved in things like:

- gate access and visitor entry
- package shelves and delivery flow
- repairs and maintenance
- sanitation and landscaping
- parking and building rules

That is why people complain about 物业, depend on it, or judge a compound through it.

## What outsiders often miss

Property management in China is not just a back-office landlord function. In many compounds it is a visible everyday interface between residents and the system around the home. It shapes how convenient, safe, strict, or frustrating a place feels.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese apartment life often extends beyond the apartment itself, 物业 is one of the key terms that explains how that domestic world is actually organized.
