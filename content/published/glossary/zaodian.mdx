## Beyond the literal

早点 is often translated as "breakfast," but the word usually points to breakfast as a local food world, not just the first meal of the day.

## What people usually mean

When people say 早点, they often mean the whole morning food scene:

- neighborhood breakfast stalls
- downstairs pickup routines
- quick hot foods before work or school
- regionally specific morning habits

## Where it appears

- signs for breakfast shops
- daily questions like **吃早点了吗**
- local food recommendations
- city talk about what mornings taste like

## What outsiders often miss

早点 is not one standard breakfast set. It changes sharply by city and region, and it often matters because it is routine, local, and hot rather than because it is elaborate.

## Why it matters

早点 opens one of the fastest ways into ordinary urban China. Morning food often tells you more about local rhythm than formal restaurant culture does.
