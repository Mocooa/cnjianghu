## What kind of word this is

寒凉 is a classification, not a temperature reading. In TCM body logic, foods and conditions are sorted along a spectrum from 热 (hot) to 寒 (cold), and what you eat is understood to shift the body's internal balance. 寒凉 foods and environments are those that move the body toward cold — which can be beneficial in some states and harmful in others.

## What counts as 寒凉

The classification covers foods, drinks, and conditions:

- **Foods**: watermelon, cucumber, pears, crab, raw salads, cold milk, most iced drinks
- **Conditions**: air conditioning, cold wind, unheated rooms, cold water on the skin during certain states
- **Behaviors**: drinking cold water first thing in the morning, eating raw food during menstruation or illness

These are not classified by their actual temperature but by their believed effect on the body's internal energy.

## Where the avoidances appear most

The strongest 寒凉 avoidances cluster around:

- **Menstruation** — cold food and drinks are thought to cause cramps and worsen flow
- **Postpartum recovery** — 坐月子 practices prohibit almost all 寒凉 exposure for a month after birth
- **Illness** — particularly digestive illness, where cold is seen as compounding the problem
- **After exertion** — drinking cold water when the body is hot is considered harmful

## What outsiders often miss

Refusing ice in a drink or asking for hot water in summer is not quirky behavior. It reflects an active, coherent body logic. The person is not being difficult — they are managing their internal state according to a framework they have held since childhood.

## Why it matters

寒凉 logic explains a set of Chinese health behaviors that are otherwise puzzling: hot water in warm weather, no ice cream after surgery, no raw vegetables during recovery. These are not superstitions — they are applications of a body theory that runs parallel to biomedical explanations.
