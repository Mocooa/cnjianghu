## What people usually mean

潜水 means staying quiet while remaining in the room. In Chinese group-chat and forum culture, it does not mean total absence. It means you are still there, just not speaking.

## Where it appears

- family and work groups
- fan communities
- forums and interest groups
- any space where presence can exist without visible participation

That is why someone can be called a **潜水成员** or be told **别潜水了**, meaning: stop staying hidden and say something.

## What outsiders often miss

潜水 is not exactly the same as "lurking" in English internet culture. In Chinese usage it often carries more social awareness. People know you are there, and your silence itself may have meaning inside the group.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese digital spaces feel socially dense even when people are quiet, 潜水 is one of the clearest small words in that logic.
