## What people usually mean

濫뽾뱦 is commonly translated as bride price, but in Chinese life it usually refers to the pre-marriage gift or payment custom associated with engagement and marriage arrangements. In contemporary practice, this can involve cash, jewelry, goods, or a larger negotiated package.

## Why it feels so charged

People may talk about 濫뽾뱦 as:

- proof of seriousness
- a family expectation
- a regional custom
- a source of pressure or resentment
- part of a larger marriage negotiation

That is why the term can sound romantic in one context and highly transactional in another.

## What outsiders often miss

濫뽾뱦 is not one uniform national rule, and it is not reducible to a simple "buying a bride" interpretation. Its meaning changes a lot by region, class position, gender expectation, and what the two families think marriage should look like.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why marriage talk in China often quickly becomes talk about parents, housing, savings, and family standing, 濫뽾뱦 is one of the clearest terms in that system.
