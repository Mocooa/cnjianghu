## What this page is

`Last-Mile System` is not a fixed everyday Chinese term in the same way as 外卖 or 快递. It is a **concept note** used here to explain the final delivery layer that people in China experience directly.

## What the system includes

In Chinese cities, the "last mile" often includes:

- riders on scooters
- apartment-compound dropoff rules
- pickup shelves and lockers
- phone calls, gate access, and building handoff
- short-distance routing shaped by platforms and neighborhood design

This is where logistics stops being backend infrastructure and becomes something visible, audible, and physical.

## What people usually mean in conversation

When people complain about, depend on, or optimize for delivery speed, they are often really talking about this last segment. It is the part of the system where expectations, labor, platform design, and urban space meet.

## What outsiders often miss

Fast delivery in China is not only a warehouse story. Much of what feels impressive or stressful happens in the final handoff layer, especially around compounds, elevators, pickup points, and rider density.

## What we should not overclaim

`Last-Mile System` is an editorial frame, not one official category used identically across every sector. But it is still a useful note because it explains why delivery in China feels like a lived urban system rather than an invisible service.
