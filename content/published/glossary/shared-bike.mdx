## What it is

共享单车 usually means a bike that you unlock through an app and use for short urban trips. In China it became especially important for quick movement between subway exits, office zones, apartment compounds, and nearby errands.

## What it solves

The shared bike matters because it fills awkward distances that are:

- too short for a car
- too long to feel trivial on foot
- too common to plan as special transport

That is why it often appears in conversations about the "last stretch" of city movement.

## What outsiders often miss

This is not just a gadgety transport option. In China, shared bikes became part of how people experience urban flow itself. They help make commuting and short-distance life feel continuous rather than broken into separate transport modes.

## Why it matters

If high-speed rail changes how people imagine long distance, 共享单车 helps explain how Chinese cities organize short distance. It is one of the simplest ways to see how platform habits, street design, and everyday movement fit together.
