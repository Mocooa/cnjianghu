## More than a literal translation

大模型 is widely used in Chinese tech and media language. English readers often hear it as a literal translation of "large model" or "large language model," but Chinese usage is broader and more public-facing than either phrase alone.

## Official and industry baseline

Chinese official communication more often regulates **generative AI services** than it defines one universal technical meaning for 大模型. In practice, policy attention has focused on filing and governing public-facing AI services, while industry talk uses 大模型 as a much wider umbrella term.

## How people actually say it

- **大模型公司** — a large-model company
- **大模型应用** — a large-model application
- **接入大模型** — integrate a large model into a product
- **多模态大模型** — a multimodal large model

## What people usually mean

In everyday Chinese tech conversation, 大模型 often refers to:

- foundation-style AI models
- domestic model releases
- competition between firms and labs
- the belief that model capability matters for national tech strength
- the product and service ecosystem built around these models

So the term behaves as both a technical label and an industrial shorthand.

## What outsiders often miss

大模型 is not just a neutral translation of "large language model." In Chinese discourse it often includes multimodal systems, productized AI services, and a broader story about capability, deployment, and competitiveness.

## Why it matters

Not every AI product in China is best understood through the lens of 大模型 alone, and not every filing notice maps cleanly onto the same technical category. But 大模型 has become one of the clearest entry terms into how China talks about AI as both technology and public competition.
