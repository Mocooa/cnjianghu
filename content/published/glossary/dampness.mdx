## What kind of word this is

湿气 is another everyday body-logic term. It does not mean literal moisture inside the body. It is a way of naming a certain bodily feel.

## What people usually mean

When people talk about 湿气, they often mean:

- heaviness
- sluggishness
- puffiness
- stickiness
- fogginess
- a sense that the body is not moving cleanly

It comes up especially in humid weather, after irregular eating or sleeping, or when someone feels weighed down rather than simply sick.

## What usually follows

In daily talk, 湿气 often leads to advice about:

- changing tea ingredients
- eating differently
- moving more
- adjusting to the season
- avoiding habits believed to make the body feel more burdened

## What outsiders often miss

湿气 is not a biomedical term, and it is not well translated by a single Western equivalent. It works as a shared shorthand for a body that feels bogged down and in need of regulation.

## Why it matters

Understanding 湿气 helps you hear Chinese health talk more accurately. Without it, ordinary conversations about food, weather, tiredness, and routine can sound irrational when they are actually following a different body logic.
