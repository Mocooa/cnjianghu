## More than energy

气 is often translated as energy. That is helpful but incomplete. In Chinese use, the word can point to vitality, internal movement, breath-like force, or the felt condition of the body.

It appears in both classical thought and ordinary conversation.

## What people usually mean

Depending on context, 气 can help describe:

- whether the body feels strong or depleted
- whether something is moving smoothly or getting blocked
- the felt force behind mood, effort, or physical condition

That is why many Chinese expressions with 气 are not easy to flatten into one English equivalent.

## What outsiders often miss

气 is not best understood as a measurable scientific substance in modern biomedical terms. But it is also not mere vague mysticism. In Chinese body talk, it remains a useful way of describing vitality and internal condition.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese body language can sound systemic rather than symptom-by-symptom, 气 is one of the central words.
