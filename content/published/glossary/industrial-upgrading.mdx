## More than getting richer

产业升级 is often rendered as industrial upgrading, but in Chinese discussion it usually points to a very specific ambition: not just more production, but better-positioned production.

The term often suggests movement away from low-margin assembly toward stronger technology, branding, precision, automation, or higher-end industrial roles.

## What people usually mean

When people talk about 产业升级, they may be referring to:

- manufacturing that becomes more automated or knowledge-intensive
- local firms moving up the value chain
- stronger control over design, components, or standards
- a region trying to shift from cheap capacity to higher-quality industry

That is why the phrase often appears in conversations about both factories and national development.

## What outsiders often miss

产业升级 is not just a neutral economics phrase. In China it often carries narrative force. It helps explain why manufacturing is discussed not only as output, but as capability, dignity, and future position.

## Why it matters

If you want to understand why "making things" in China is often discussed as a story of transformation rather than simple scale, 产业升级 is one of the key entry terms.
