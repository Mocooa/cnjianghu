## More than a holiday meal

年夜饭 refers to the New Year's Eve meal for Lunar New Year. In Chinese life, it often carries more emotional weight than the English phrase "reunion dinner" suggests.

It is not only about eating well. It is also about showing up, returning home, and marking the year through food.

## What people usually mean

When people talk about 年夜饭, they usually mean some combination of:

- the most important family meal of the year
- dishes chosen for symbolism as well as taste
- return-home pressure and reunion logistics
- a moment where family roles become especially visible

That is why the dinner can feel warm, stressful, abundant, and obligatory at the same time.

## What outsiders often miss

Foreign readers sometimes imagine reunion dinner as simply "Chinese Thanksgiving." That misses the specific timing and emotional structure. 年夜饭 sits inside Lunar New Year return-home culture, ideas of filial presence, and a strong expectation that family togetherness should be materially visible through food.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand how food, family, timing, and obligation come together in Chinese festival life, 年夜饭 is one of the strongest entry points.
