## What it is

In current Chinese livestream culture, PK usually means a timed battle between streamers. The platforms turn competition into a built-in interaction format.

## What it changes

Once a room enters PK mode, viewers are pushed to respond together:

- they rally behind one host
- they send gifts
- they watch rankings shift in real time
- they treat the room like a live event rather than a passive stream

## Where it appears

- entertainment livestreams
- multi-host rooms
- short-video live platforms
- creator competitions built around traffic and gifts

## What outsiders often miss

PK is not just "versus." In Chinese streaming it is a platform mechanic with social consequences. It shapes pacing, loyalty, pressure, and the emotional temperature of a room.

## Why it matters

PK shows how Chinese platforms often turn interaction into format. A simple battle feature becomes a repeatable engine for participation and monetization.
