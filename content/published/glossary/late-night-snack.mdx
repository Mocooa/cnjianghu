## More than food at a late hour

邏런쉇 literally means a late-night snack, but in Chinese life it often means more than eating after dark. It can point to a whole mood of staying out, slowing down, meeting friends, or recovering from a long workday.

## Where it shows up

You are likely to hear 邏런쉇 in scenes like:

- after-work dinners that start too late to count as dinner
- post-barbecue or hotpot add-on meals
- delivery orders placed after study or overtime
- night-market wandering that ends with "let's get something"

## What outsiders often miss

Late-night eating in China is not always framed as private indulgence. 邏런쉇 is often social, recreational, and urban. The point may be as much about lingering together or re-entering the city at night as about appetite itself.

## Why it matters

If daytime Chinese eating can reveal routine and efficiency, 邏런쉇 often reveals the softer side of city life: fatigue, companionship, loosened schedules, and the feeling that the day is not fully over yet.
