## Beyond the obvious translation

`Immortal` is the usual English shortcut for 仙, but it can flatten the word too quickly. In Chinese contexts, 仙 often implies not just living forever, but attaining a transformed state through cultivation, discipline, or cosmic alignment.

## Where it appears

- mythology
- Daoist-inflected stories
- fantasy novels and dramas
- games and animation
- everyday references to "fairy-like" beauty or otherworldliness

## What outsiders often miss

An 仙 is not simply the Chinese version of a Greek god or a vampire-like immortal. The word often sits inside a larger system of mountains, sects, training, transcendence, and moral ambiguity.

## Why it matters

If Chinese fantasy and mythology feel structurally different from Western fantasy, 仙 is one of the key words behind that difference.
