## What this page is

`Lying Flat` is a **concept note** for foreign readers. It helps explain the English-facing phrase people latch onto before they fully understand the denser Chinese term `Tangping`.

## What people usually mean in this archive context

In this archive, `Lying Flat` points to a recognizable social move:

- stepping back from endless striving
- lowering ambition when the script feels unwinnable
- preserving energy rather than maximizing achievement
- refusing to let pressure define the entire meaning of life

That is why the phrase often appears in debates about work, housing, marriage, and youth expectation.

## What outsiders often miss

`Lying Flat` can sound in English like total surrender. But in the Chinese context, the stronger idea is often selective withdrawal rather than complete collapse.

The move may be quiet, limited, or tactical. It does not necessarily mean giving up on life itself.

## Why it matters

If foreigners keep seeing "lying flat" in writing about Chinese youth, this concept note helps them enter the larger life-script debate without mistaking the phrase for simple laziness.
