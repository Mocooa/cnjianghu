## What people usually mean

给面子 means giving someone public consideration in a way that confirms their standing. At the table, this can happen through:

- accepting an invitation
- responding to a toast
- taking a seat arrangement seriously
- not refusing too bluntly in front of others
- showing up when presence itself matters

## Why it matters at meals

Banquets and hosted meals are one of the clearest places where 给面子 becomes visible. Food is not the only thing being exchanged. People are also acknowledging rank, relationship, and effort.

## What outsiders often miss

This is not just flattery. Nor is it simply "being nice." Giving face often means recognizing the social situation and helping it hold together in public. That is why what looks small from the outside can feel significant in context.

## Why it matters

If 面子 is the broader logic of publicly legible standing, 给面子 is one of the actions through which that logic gets maintained in real life.
