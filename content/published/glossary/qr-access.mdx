## What this page is

`QR Access` is a **concept note** for a pattern in Chinese urban life where scanning a code often does more than complete a payment.

It can also act as a way to enter, verify, unlock, register, or move forward in a process.

## What people usually mean in this archive context

In this archive, `QR Access` covers situations where scanning becomes the practical doorway to:

- entering services or digital queues
- opening mini-program or service flows
- confirming pickup or delivery handoff
- joining a venue, event, or managed urban routine

That is why QR codes in China often feel like access mechanisms, not just transaction tools.

## What outsiders often miss

Foreign readers may notice lots of codes without realizing that the deeper shift is behavioral: people become used to treating scanning as the expected first action.

So the code is not just visual clutter. It is a compact interface for moving between offline space and platform-managed action.

## What we should not overclaim

`QR Access` is not one official policy term, and not every use of a QR code belongs in the same category.

But it remains a useful bridge concept because it explains why Chinese cities can feel unusually ready to turn one small scan into a whole next step.
