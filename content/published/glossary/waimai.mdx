## What it is

外卖 means takeaway or food delivery, but in Chinese city life it usually refers to the full delivery habit rather than just a single order.

## What people usually mean

When people say they are "ordering 外卖," they may be talking about:

- lunch at the office
- late-night food at home
- milk tea or fruit
- a quick meal between other tasks

The word often implies app-based convenience, timing, and urban routine.

## What it changes

外卖 is not only a way to get food. It changes where eating happens, how cravings are handled, and how city life feels from inside apartments, offices, dorms, and compounds.

## What outsiders often miss

外卖 is not just laziness or emergency food. In many Chinese cities it behaves more like ordinary infrastructure: expected, fast, and built into daily rhythm.

## Why it matters

If you want to understand modern urban appetite in China, 外卖 explains a huge part of it. It is one of the clearest examples of convenience becoming culture.
