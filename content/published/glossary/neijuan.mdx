## What kind of word this is

内卷 is one of the clearest Chinese slang terms for structural pressure. It sounds compact, but it carries a lot of social diagnosis.

## What people usually mean

When people say something is 内卷, they usually mean:

- competition keeps intensifying
- more work is required just to stay in place
- individual effort no longer feels fairly rewarded
- everyone is pushed to optimize because everyone else is doing the same

That is why the word often appears in talk about school, work, parenting, hiring, and status anxiety.

## Where it appears

- office complaints
- student life
- online jokes about overwork
- conversations about careers and family expectations

## What outsiders often miss

内卷 is not just the academic word "involution" dropped into casual Chinese. In ordinary use, it is a lived feeling of pressure, crowding, and diminishing returns.

## Why it matters

内卷 helps explain why so much contemporary Chinese slang feels socially dense. A single word can summarize how structural strain becomes everyday emotional weather.
