## What kind of word this is

补 is not just a nutrition word. It belongs to the broader Chinese body-and-food worldview in which meals can strengthen, restore, and rebalance.

## What people usually mean

When someone says a person needs 补一补, they usually mean the body seems depleted:

- after illness
- after childbirth
- after long fatigue
- in certain seasons
- whenever someone looks run down

This is why soups, slow-cooked broths, and certain ingredients are often described as 补.

## How people actually say it

- **补身体** — to strengthen the body
- **进补** — to take in nourishing foods or tonics
- **大补** — strongly replenishing or tonifying

## What outsiders often miss

补 is not the same thing as "healthy" in a generic sense. It is more targeted than that. It implies restoring what seems spent, thin, weak, or overdrawn.

## Why it matters

补 helps explain why Chinese food talk often moves easily between flavor, care, and bodily effect. A nourishing dish is not only tasty; it is also doing work on the body.
