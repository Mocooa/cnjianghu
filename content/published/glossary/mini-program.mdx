## What it is

小程序 are built-in services that run inside a platform instead of asking users to download a separate standalone app. In practice, the term usually points to **WeChat Mini Programs**.

## What people usually mean

When people in China mention a 小程序, they often mean a fast way to do something ordinary:

- order food
- book tickets
- pay a bill
- register for a service
- join a small workflow without leaving WeChat

## What it changes

The point is not only that a 小程序 is "lighter" than an app. It also reduces friction. Because people are already inside WeChat, many services can become part of one existing social and payment environment.

## What outsiders often miss

小程序 are not just smaller apps. In China they helped make one platform feel like a daily operating layer. That is why WeChat can feel less like a messenger and more like infrastructure.

## Why it matters

If you want to understand why Chinese digital life often feels more integrated than Western app life, 小程序 is one of the clearest mechanisms behind that difference.
