## What kind of word this is

破防 is internet slang with emotional charge. It is often playful on the surface but points to a real moment of being hit, embarrassed, or exposed.

## What people usually mean

Someone can 破防 when:

- a joke lands too hard
- an emotional scene hits unexpectedly
- a criticism feels uncomfortably accurate
- a truth is too close to home

That is why it often sits halfway between joking and confession.

## How people actually say it

- **我破防了** — I cracked
- **别破防** — don't get so pressed / don't let it get to you
- **这也能破防** — that was enough to crack you?

## What outsiders often miss

破防 is not perfectly captured by "triggered." It often suggests that someone's emotional defenses have been punctured, while still staying within an online, socially legible register.

## Why it matters

破防 shows how Chinese internet slang can carry both irony and sincerity at once. A joke word can still point to real vulnerability.
