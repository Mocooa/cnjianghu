## Beyond the literal

Taken literally, 人情 looks soft: human feeling, human sentiment. In practice, it is softer than a rule but heavier than a feeling.

## How it works

人情 is created when people help, show up, introduce, cover for, gift, host, or otherwise invest in a relationship. That is why people say:

- **欠人情** — to owe someone a favor
- **还人情** — to repay that social debt
- **讲人情** — to act with human consideration rather than cold procedure

It often does not demand instant repayment, but it does create memory.

## Where it shows up

- Weddings and funerals
- Business introductions
- Family and hometown networks
- Hosting and gift exchange
- Moments when formal rules are not enough

## What outsiders often miss

人情 is not just "kindness." It is a relationship mechanism. It helps explain why favors in China are often remembered for a long time and why help can come bundled with future expectation.

## Why it matters

If 面子 explains public standing, 人情 explains relational debt. Together they show how many Chinese relationships are maintained not just through emotion, but through remembered acts of care, help, and obligation.
