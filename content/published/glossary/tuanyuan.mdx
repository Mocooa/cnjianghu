## What kind of idea this is

团圆 is not just an event. It is a value — the belief that family should be physically gathered, especially at festival time. The character 圆 means round or complete, and that roundness is the point: the family circle closed, everyone present, nothing missing.

## How it organizes behavior

The pull of 团圆 explains some of the most visible logistical phenomena in China:

- The Spring Festival migration — 春运 — is the largest annual human movement on earth, driven by people trying to get home for 团圆
- Reunion dinner on New Year's Eve — 年夜饭 — is considered incomplete if family members are absent
- Round foods (tangyuan, dumplings, mooncakes) appear at festival tables specifically because the shape echoes the ideal of wholeness

## Where it shows up most

团圆 peaks at Spring Festival, Mid-Autumn Festival, and the Lantern Festival. But the idea operates year-round. Guilt about missing a family gathering, anxiety about a parent eating alone, the weight placed on the New Year's table — all of it runs on the logic of 团圆.

## What outsiders often miss

团圆 is not just warm sentiment. It carries social obligation. Being absent from a reunion often requires explanation or apology. For young people working far from home or choosing not to return, 团圆 can feel like pressure as much as comfort.

## Why it matters

Understanding 团圆 helps explain why Chinese festivals center so heavily on travel and food rather than public ritual alone. The table at home is the ceremony.
