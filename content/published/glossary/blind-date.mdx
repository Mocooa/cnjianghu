## What people usually mean

相亲 is commonly translated as blind date, but in Chinese life it often means a more socially legible kind of introduction arranged through family, friends, coworkers, or matchmaking channels.

## Why it is bigger than a one-off date

In practice, 相亲 often comes with assumptions about:

- seriousness
- marriage timing
- family background
- work and housing
- whether the match is practical as well as emotionally possible

That is why people can talk about **相亲对象** as a distinct relationship category rather than just "someone I met once."

## What outsiders often miss

相亲 is not simply an old-fashioned leftover or proof that romance is absent. In contemporary China it often works as one legitimate route into dating and marriage, especially when family timelines, work pressure, and social filtering all matter.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese dating talk so quickly touches parents, housing, and long-term viability, 相亲 is one of the clearest terms in that system.
