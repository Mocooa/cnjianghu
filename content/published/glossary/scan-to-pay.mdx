## What it is

扫码支付 means paying by scanning a QR code, usually through Alipay or WeChat Pay. In China it became normal across both formal and informal retail settings.

## What it changes

Because the same payment habit works in so many places, scan-to-pay reduces friction across:

- street stalls
- convenience stores
- restaurants
- taxis
- neighborhood services

## What outsiders often miss

This is not just a flashy payment feature. In China, scan-to-pay became part of baseline urban routine, which is why cashless life can feel less like a premium option and more like environmental default.

## Why it matters

If foreign visitors say paying in China feels unusually frictionless, 扫码支付 is usually the first mechanism they are noticing.
