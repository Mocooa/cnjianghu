## More than destiny

缘分 is often translated as fate, but that is too broad and too heavy. The word usually points to a connection that feels meaningful because of timing, encounter, and affinity, not because everything was rigidly predetermined.

## What people usually mean

When people talk about 缘分, they may be referring to:

- meeting the right person at a particular moment
- a connection that feels strangely natural
- a missed or partial connection that still "counts"
- the idea that not all important relationships can be planned into existence

That is why 缘分 can sound romantic, but it is not limited to romance.

## What outsiders often miss

缘分 is not identical to soulmates, destiny, or luck. It leaves room for contingency and incompleteness. Two people can have 缘分 and still not stay together. The word often marks significance without guaranteeing permanence.

## Why it matters

If foreigners want to understand why Chinese discussions of timing and connection can feel softer than strict fate but denser than mere coincidence, 缘分 is one of the key words.
