## What this page is

高考 is a real and common term, but for foreign readers it works best as a **concept note** because the exam itself is only part of what people mean when they talk about it.

## Official baseline

At the most formal level, 高考 refers to the **national college entrance examination** for regular higher-education admissions. The Ministry of Education defines it as a national unified exam set for ordinary university admissions.

## What people usually mean in conversation

In everyday Chinese use, 高考 often means more than the exam papers themselves. It can also point to:

- years of family pressure
- school competition
- a sorting mechanism for life chances
- a moral script about effort and endurance

That is why people may use 高考 as shorthand for an entire stage of life.

## What outsiders often miss

It is inaccurate to reduce 高考 to "China's SAT." The scale, social concentration, and emotional weight are different. The exam sits inside a broader structure of schooling, family expectation, and mobility.

## What we should not overclaim

高考 does not explain every educational outcome in China by itself, and local policies, provincial score lines, and university admissions rules all matter. But as a public symbol, it remains one of the most powerful organizing ideas in modern Chinese student life.
