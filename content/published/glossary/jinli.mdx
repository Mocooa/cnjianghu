## What kind of word this is

锦鲤 has been a Chinese symbol of good fortune for centuries. The carp that leaps the dragon gate and transforms into a dragon is one of China's most enduring metaphors for perseverance rewarded. But since 2018, the word has taken on a second life as internet slang meaning someone with absurd, enviable luck.

## How it became internet slang

In 2018, Alipay ran a massive giveaway and crowned one winner as "China's Jinli." The campaign went viral, and the term exploded across social media. Now calling someone 锦鲤 means they seem to attract good fortune effortlessly — winning draws, acing exams, landing jobs against the odds.

## How people actually use it

People repost 锦鲤 images before exams, job interviews, and lottery draws. The tone is half-joking, half-serious — nobody fully believes reposting a fish picture will change their fate, but nobody wants to be the one who skipped it either. The phrase "转发这条锦鲤" (repost this jinli) became a formula for pre-event rituals online.

## What outsiders often miss

The term blends traditional symbolism with internet humor in a way that is distinctly Chinese. The ancient carp myth is not being mocked — it is being remixed. The same person who reposts a 锦鲤 meme may also visit a temple before an important exam. These layers of belief and play coexist without contradiction.

## Why it matters

锦鲤 shows how Chinese internet culture takes ancient symbols and gives them new functions. It is a good example of how tradition does not simply survive in modern China — it gets repurposed, often in ways that are playful but still carry real cultural weight.
