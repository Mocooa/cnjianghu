## More than a physical edge

门槛 literally means a threshold or door sill, but in Chinese use it often extends beyond architecture. It can also suggest a barrier, an entry condition, or a meaningful boundary between inside and outside.

## Why it matters in space

In built form, a threshold is not always just a practical transition. It can signal:

- a change of social role
- a change in intimacy
- a shift from public to private
- the need to pause, adjust, or acknowledge where you are entering

## What outsiders often miss

Foreign readers may treat thresholds as neutral architectural details. In Chinese settings, thresholds can carry etiquette, sequencing, and emotional temperature. The way a space is entered often matters as much as the space itself.

## Why it matters

If Chinese architecture and interiors often feel layered rather than abruptly open, 门槛 is one of the reasons. It helps explain how spatial design and social logic can overlap.
